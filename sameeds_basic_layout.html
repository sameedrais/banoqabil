Speaking Skills


Speaking:

“the action of conveying information or expressing one's feelings in speech.”


Fluency
How comfortable and confident you are in speaking any language (e.g. English).
Vocabulary
Being a good speaker means constantly growing your vocabulary. The more interesting words you know, the stronger your speaking skills would be.
Grammar
Grammar does matter, however; fewer mistakes you are allowed to make. But a good speaker does not have to use perfect grammer.
Pronunciation
It's a complex area that needs practice. A skilled speaker knows the sub-skills of pronunciation that include; word stress, intonation, and rhythm.

Why is it important?

Because it enhances your cognitive skills including:

  1. presenting an argument
  2. justifying an argument
  3. asking questions

Public Speaking

  • Informative Speaking
  • Persuasive Speaking





Informative Speaking

An informative speech is like a presentation where the goal is to increase others’ knowledge understanding, or abilities. For instance, you might give an informative speech that aims to increase listeners’ understanding of how science works and to teach them what are the wonders of science.

Persuasive Speaking

Most of us won’t give persuasive speeches regularly but in some cases, we are asked to make persuasive presentations. Its goal is to change others by prompting them to think, feel, believe, or act differently. For instance, your manager might want you to persuade a potential client that your firm can provide the best service, so you speak to persuade others to ideas or actions that you think are right or desirable.
